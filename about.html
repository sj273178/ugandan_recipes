Cultures & Regions

Food Rooted in Uganda's Many Cultures

Uganda is home to over 56 indigenous tribes, each with their own language, traditions and — most importantly — their own food. From the Baganda of the central region to the Acholi of the north and the Banyankole of the southwest, every community brings something unique to the national table.

In the east, the Bagisu celebrate with Malewa — bamboo shoots slow-cooked with groundnuts. In the west, the Banyankole take pride in milk-based dishes and roasted meats. Along the shores of Lake Victoria, the Basoga and Baganda communities have built a cuisine around fresh tilapia and Nile perch.

At TasteUganda, we journey across all these regions to document the dishes, the stories behind them and the people who keep these traditions alive.
